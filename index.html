Meeting Minutes: What to Eat?

This meeting was called to discuss the majorly important and controversial topic of what food we should get.
We will begin by each listing our top three choices, then using this data we will determine the best choice.

Green's Choices

  1. Thai
  2. Indian
  3. Poke

Gage's Choices

  1. Burgers
  2. Burritos
  3. Indian

Cameron's Choices

  1. Poke
  2. Indian
  3. Burgers

Data Extrapolation

To now determine the best choice based on our given lists we must assign points to our rankings.
We could simply assign rank one choices to have 3 points then rank two with 2 points and rank 3 with 1 point,
however it will be better to keep a more even value split to prioritize choices shared among the lists.
Therefore we will have a point split of 5-4-3 which will give us the rankings of:

  1. Indian - 12
  2. Poke - 8
  3. Burgers - 8
  4. Thai - 5
  5. Burritos - 4

Conclusions

With all points tallied we have concluded that Indian food is what we will be getting.
With that settled our next meeting we will discuss what we will be ordering from said Indian restaurant.
This meeting will be held in roughly 30 minutes when we arrive at the restaurant with menus in hand.
